SECOND-PASS AUDIT · 18 IDEAS · 12 SHIPPED · REVISED JULY 2026

You closed the rings. Here's the frontier.

The first audit was about everything around the craft — getting in, getting out, not losing work. That's largely done. So this one goes straight at the craft itself: the industry standards you don't yet speak, the game-ready output real engines demand, the whole disciplines (the face, colour, staging) that aren't in the suite yet — and a short list of things that, if you ship them, make people screenshot your app. None of it needs a server.

⚗ THE ONE-LINE VERDICT

PolyPotion is now a complete workshop that speaks mostly its own language. The next leap isn't more tools — it's fluency in the formats and disciplines the rest of the 3D world already uses, so a character made here drops cleanly into VRChat, Unity, Unreal, an iPhone's face camera, or a headset without a detour through Blender. Teach the suite those dialects and the 25 tools you built stop being a clever demo and become someone's actual pipeline.

✔ Revision note — both brews are shipped. Retarget is a new tool in the animate dock (auto bone-map for Mixamo / UE5 / Rigify / VRM + fuzzy fallback, hip-scale root correction, GLB export). Morph grew a one-click ARKit 52 set. The Export dialog speaks VRM 1.0 (humanoid map + expressions from the ARKit names). Bake Maps packs ORM and takes an OpenGL/DirectX normal toggle. Showcase has a WebXR view-in-your-room button. The second brew followed: bone naming on export, LOD chains, an expression board, a unit pre-flight, and the character passport. A closing pattern worth naming: nearly everything here is a translation layer, not a new engine. You already generate humanoids, paint weights, sculpt morphs, sim cloth, bake maps and record turntables. VRM, ARKit shapes, retargeting, LODs, colour management — these mostly re-label and re-package work the suite already does into dialects the wider world reads. That's the cheapest kind of leverage there is: new audiences, almost no new math.

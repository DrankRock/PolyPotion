CHAPTER V · FULL-SOLUTION SWEEP · JULY 19, 2026

The shelf is stocked — and two doors are nailed shut

Thirty-one tools, shared contracts for navigation, symmetry, colour and jobs, a finished VTuber pipeline. The craft holds. But this sweep found that the two flagship toys — Playground and Stream Stage — are currently unreachable from the shell, that offline has been silently broken since the CDN migration, and that the app still introduces itself with a year-old What's New. None of it is engine rot. All of it is the cost of six hand-maintained tool lists — now seven, and the seventh bit.

The ordering writes itself. First the two nailed-shut doors — both are one-line list fixes and they resurrect the flagships. Then the registry consolidation, because it makes this entire class of bug impossible and it's the foundation the planned workspace presets already assume. Then make the offline promise honest: CDN hosts in the service worker, the engine precache, real icons. Everything in the horizon section is optional; everything above it is the difference between what the README claims and what a new user gets. And then the circus: the three rings below the horizon are the ambition ladder — ring one makes PolyPotion a place where things are BORN (keyframe authoring, bundled motion, dress-up, face-from-photo, sprites), ring two takes the VTuber lane outright (hands, true-alpha overlays, Japanese), ring three turns a tool into a place (version history, batch work, sharable .potion atoms, a community shelf, quests, a front door). None of it needs a server. All of it composes from engines already shipped — which is the whole review in one sentence: the hard parts are done, the circus is assembly.

The four rules of joint placement
  • Center of the volume, always. A joint is a rotation pivot — it sits in the middle of the limb's thickness (the medial axis), never on the surface. Check the side view: a knee that looks right from the front is often floating in front of the leg.
  • Anatomical pivots, not visual middles. Place where the real bone rotates: the hip socket, the elbow crease, ear level for the head.
  • Rig in T-pose. Mixamo clips assume a T-pose rest (arms straight out, palms down). If your mesh is A-posed, retargeted arms will land wrong.
  • Bulky characters: center on the bone line, not the muscle mass. A huge deltoid doesn't move the shoulder pivot outward — it stays close to the torso. Use Auto-center after rough placement, then sanity-check shoulders and hips yourself.
Core
Hips (root)
Center of the pelvis, midway between the two hip sockets — roughly where the leg crease tops out. Centered front-to-back. Not the belly button, not the waist.
Spine
A hand above the hips, at belly level. Push it slightly toward the back — the real spine is behind the gut, especially on a bulky torso.
Chest
Sternum height, centered in the ribcage. This bone carries the shoulders, so keep it on the body's midline.
Neck
Where the neck meets the shoulders — the pit of the throat, centered in the neck's thickness.
Head
The skull's pivot: ear level, centered between the ears. Too low and nods hinge at the chin; too high and the whole head shears.
Arms
Shoulder
Inside the deltoid, at the arm's rotation center — a little below the shoulder's top surface and in from its outer edge. The most common mistake is placing it at the top corner of the silhouette: that stretches the whole armpit when the arm raises.
Elbow
Dead center of the elbow's thickness, at the crease. On a muscular arm, center between bicep and tricep — not inside the bicep bulge.
Wrist
At the wrist crease, centered in the wrist. Everything from here out is hand — if it's too far up the forearm, the forearm bends like rubber.
Legs
Upper leg
The hip socket: inside the top of the thigh, NOT at the pelvis center — that's the Hips joint's job. Front-to-back, center of the thigh.
Knee
Center of the knee's volume, nudged slightly forward (toward the kneecap). A dead-center knee locks oddly when bent deep.
Ankle
At ankle-bone height, centered in the ankle — which means slightly back, above the heel, not above the arch.
Toe
The ball of the foot — where it flexes when you walk. Inside the foot, not at the tip of the toes.
Hands
Finger bases (knuckles)
Each at its knuckle center, inside the finger's thickness. Crank mesh opacity to 100% and use Focus → Hands; the depth (top view) matters as much as the front view.
Thumb
The thumb base sits low, near the wrist, and the chain angles forward out of the palm — it's the one finger that isn't flat with the hand.

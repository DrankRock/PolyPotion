CHAPTER IV · FACE RIGGING & THE VTUBER TEST · JULY 18, 2026

The face audit — great bones, dead eyes, no stage

The face stack is genuinely strong: full ARKit-52 authoring with landmark-guided synthesis, on-device webcam capture that speaks the same 52 names, three-tier lipsync, and a real VRM 1.0 exporter. The architecture bet — standardise everything on ARKit names — was the right one, and it's why every fix below is small. But run the VTuber test end-to-end and it fails in four places: the exported avatar has dead eyes in VSeeFace, no PerfectSync, hair that's frozen solid, and — the big one — there is no way to actually go live.

The ordering writes itself. Fix the exporter first — look-at presets, PerfectSync clips, VRM 0.x, head annotation are all small edits to one file and they turn every already-made character into a working VSeeFace avatar. Then turn on head pose and recording in capture (two flags and a buffer). Spring bones are the one real engineering job. And the Stream Stage is the prize: the suite already owns every ingredient — capture, avatar, backgrounds, an iframe shell — nobody has assembled them into "go live from a browser tab" before.
